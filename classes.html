Creative Writing Classes

Through the courtesy of the Surrey Arts Council, I will be the writer in residence at the new Newton Cultural Centre at 13530 72nd Ave, Surrey, BC, V3W 2P1. I will be there every Monday from 10 AM to noon.

This centre is near the intersection of King George and 72nd Ave. It’s in a converted fire hall, across from McDonalds. There’s ample parking, but it’s best to approach the centre from the west, since 72nd has a barrier in the middle of the street there. For example, enter 72nd from 132nd Street and drive toward King George. It’ll be on your right, just before King George.

All are welcome. Bring your stories, articles, novels, poems, whatever and I will try to help you toward publication. No appointment necessary. Come for the writing, but enjoy the art.

All of these courses count toward your diploma in creative writing, issued by the Fraser Valley Writers School, Ed Griffin and David Blinkhorn. One hour of class time = one credit toward a diploma. Sixty (60) hours, credits, needed for a diploma. Right now the only required course is the Introduction to Creative Writing.

All of these courses are at the Newton Cultural Centre.

Introduction to the Graphic Novel:
Do you want to write a comic or a graphic novel?
This four-week course will introduce: techniques of creating, developing, and writing the graphic novel, manga, and other forms of illustrated writing. Students will develop their own ideas into graphic form and workshop those ideas with class. In-class exercises will develop techniques in illustrated writing and provide the groundwork to take the projects to the next level. Drawing abilities are not essential; the only necessity is your creativity. Required text:  Making Comics: Storytelling Secrets of Comics, Manga and Graphic Novels, by Scott McCloud (New York: HarperCollins, 2006). Suggested materials: pencils, eraser, pens (inks) and paper (loose-leaf or sketchbook). Work will be done during and outside of class time.

Cost:$125
Instructor: Andrea Hoff
Dates and Times: Saturdays 1:00 – 3:00 pm, September 22 – October 20, 2012 (no class on October 6, 2012)

Character:
Character is one of the cornerstones of great writing. Characters reveal themselves in the way they act, speak, think and look. In this twelve hour course learn how to bring your characters alive, create believable and interesting actors for your story through the uses of senses and emotions. Topics will include credibility, purpose, complexity, methods of character presentation, and development of secondary and minor characters. This course consists of lecture, discussion, writing and critiquing.

Cost: $150 (includes lunch each day)
Instructor: David Blinkhorn
Dates and Times: Saturdays, 10:00 am – 2:30 pm December 1, 8, and 15, 2012

Publishing Courses:

Making the Zine:
What is a zine?
“A zine (/ˈziːn/ zeen; an abbreviation of fanzine, or magazine) is most commonly a small circulation publication of original or appropriated texts and images. More broadly, the term encompasses any self-published work of minority interest usually reproduced via photocopier… Zines are written in a variety of formats, from computer-printed text to comics to handwritten text.” from wickipedia (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zine)
Zine’s can be funny, serious or both. They can be about your life, your interests or something completely made up. This one-day class will explore techniques and materials involved in making a zine. Students will leave with at least one edition of their own zine, ready to reproduce and distribute to the world at large. Bring your passion and your ideas. All work will be done in class time.

Cost: $60
Instructor: Andrea Hoff
Date: Saturday, September 15, 2012 10:00 – 2:30 pm

Freelance Magazine and Article Writing:
Getting your magazine or newspaper article published can be the first step in being able to make a living as a writer. Getting your work out there is not as difficult as you think. All you need is a few basic skills and to learn a few tricks of the trade.

This fast-paced class, will cover:
Effective interviewing skills—Learn how to get thoughtful, insightful information from people and still make your deadline!
Finding credible sources— Learn the research skills necessary to find trusted information.
Putting the “creative” in your non-fiction—The days of dry boring non-fiction are gone. Learn techniques to turn your article into an engaging story.
What editors want and expect—What drives editors crazy and what makes them want to work with you.
Where to publish—In this world, there are so many places looking for good copy. Find your niche.
This course will be a combination of lecture, exercises, and workshop. By the end of this class, the students will submit an article for publication at a magazine or newspaper of their choosing.

Cost: $95
Instructor: Leslie Dyson
Dates and Times: Saturdays 10:00 a.m. – 12:00 p.m. October 27, and November 3, 10, and 17 2012

Performance and Readings

The Dynamic Presentor: Engaging a *Listening* Audience with Your Written Words:
This voice and performance skills workshop for writers and poets is divided into two parts. You will be taught techniques and enjoyable exercises that will allow you to relax, develop your voice and increase your power of projection making you a more dynamic speaker.
The second part of this two week workshop will focus on how to read your written words in an engaging way to transform your poetry/book reading into an event to be remembered. The workshop will present many different strategies and practical advice on: selecting the best poems/stories to read out loud, breaking through nerves/anxiety and knowing/targeting your audience. There will be an opportunity for everyone to practice, and share their readings with the group.
Cost: $ 60
Instructor: Ben Nuttall-Smith
