About Once A Priest

    Young Father Griffin marches from Selma to Montgomery with Dr. Martin Luther King. He feels alive, living his religion. When he returns to his all-white parish in a suburb of Cleveland, Ohio, he discovers that some parishioners have threatened the pastor of the parish, his superior. “Get that nigger-lover out of here or we’ll never contribute another dime.”
    Griffin is moved to a church in Cleveland’s slums. He’s hurt. His church has not stood behind him. His disillusionment grows as he sees the church’s indifference to the problems of the poor. He leaves the priesthood in 1968.
    Does he abandon his priesthood? Forget about helping people? No, but he eliminates the vocabulary of religion and searches for what ennobles and empowers people. He helps young people sort through life’s problems. He serves a term as a city councilman in Milwaukee, builds a successful business and teaches in prison. Everywhere he tries to lead people to a higher level. In marriage, he finally discovers the love he preached about as a priest. He learns what it means to be a ‘father’ by really being one.
    Griffin lives the spirit of ministry, stripped of religion and exercised in the world. Others who love the work, but suffer under institutional rules, will see hope in his life. Any reader who has wrestled with helping others will enjoy Griffin’s spiritual journey.

The book includes:
  • In the seminary Griffin is conservative, a favorite of the faculty. His best friend is his opposite, a freethinking seminarian, Charlie.
  • Griffin is ordained, but doesn’t really become a Christian until the civil rights movement forces him to.
  • He meets and marches with Dr. Martin Luther King in Selma.
  • Some people in his suburban parish put pressure on the bishop to get rid of Griffin. The bishop moves him to Cleveland’s poorest area, where he learns how little the church actually cares about racism and poverty. He leaves the priesthood in 1968.
  • A thirty-two virgin starts to date. He knows nothing about dating and very little about women.
  • Griffin meets his future wife and they get to know each other as they knock on 11,000 doors in his political campaign.
  • As the election draws closer, Griffin needs to get one more brochure out, but he doesn’t have the money. A reputed Mafia bar owner meets with him and hands him $900 under the table. Griffin accepts it without a qualm, until……….
  • Griffin is elected and hopes to help people
  • After four years he and his wife and now -- two small children -- throw caution to the wind and buy a commercial greenhouse, a business they know nothing about.
  • Thirteen years later they do it again, something wild, that is. Griffin is 52 then. They leave their native land and a profitable business and move across North America to British Columbia, Canada.
  • In BC he starts to teach writing, first one class, then another and another. Writing groups form. People get together and they establish the Surrey International Writers' Conference. Griffin also starts teaching writing in prison in 1993.
  • The doctor says he has cancer in 1996. His world is shattered. The doctor operates and then tells Griffin. "Sorry, Ed, we didn't get it all." Griffin learns to live with cancer
  • He experiences a second spring, a spiritual renewal.
